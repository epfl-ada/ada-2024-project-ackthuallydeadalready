Ackthually Dead Already

Public sentiments on Wikipedia admins and their consequences in RFA elections: A deep dive into voting patterns, community behavior, and sentiment trends shaping the Wikipedia admin landscape.

Imagine a world where trust isn't just a virtue but a cornerstone of governance. On Wikipedia, the administrators—those entrusted with safeguarding the platform's integrity—embark on a journey to earn this trust through a social consensus process called Request for Adminship (RfA). Unlike the conventional voting systems we encounter in politics or workplaces, the RfA is more than just a tally of yeses and nos. It's a nuanced conversation where every comment, question, and response shapes the community's decision.

Now, what if we could peek into this dynamic process, unraveling the sentiments, themes, and turning points that sway opinions? Welcome to our project. By combining the art of sentiment analysis with cutting-edge natural language processing, we aim to decode the fabric of consensus-building in Wikipedia's admin elections. From policy stances to communication skills, we will uncover the topics that ignite the most spirited debates.

But that's not all. Just as a keen observer tracks the evolution of actors' careers, we'll analyze how community discourse evolves over time. Are certain topics fading into obscurity, or do they remain central to the decision-making process? How do individual voters engage with contentious issues? And can we predict outcomes by examining the underlying dynamics of these discussions?

Join us as we explore the rich social dynamics of Wikipedia's RfA process, offering insights not just into the world of online governance but also into designing more effective systems of collective decision-making.
